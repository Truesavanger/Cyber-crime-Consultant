Protect Yourself: How to Prevent Phishing Attacks

Be Skeptical of Emails

Avoid clicking on links or opening attachments from unsolicited emails. Verify the sender's legitimacy, especially if the email urges immediate action or contains unexpected requests.

Stay Informed on Smishing and Vishing

Be cautious of unexpected SMS messages or phone calls, especially those pressuring you to share personal information. Verify the source independently before responding to such communications.

Enhance Email Security

Implement robust email security measures, including spam filters and email authentication protocols. Educate employees and users about recognizing phishing attempts and reporting suspicious emails.

Regularly Update Security Software

Ensure that your antivirus and anti-malware software is up-to-date. Regularly update your operating system and applications to patch vulnerabilities that attackers may exploit.

Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Implement MFA to add an extra layer of security. Even if attackers obtain login credentials, MFA requires additional verification, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.
